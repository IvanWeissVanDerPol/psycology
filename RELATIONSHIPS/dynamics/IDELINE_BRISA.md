## Psychological Insights

### 1. The Fixer Emerges Fast

Within weeks of meeting, Ivan established himself as:
- The one who cooks
- The one who has information
- The one who waits and is available
- The one who offers to fix problems

This suggests The Fixer isn't developed gradually - it's Ivan's **default mode** for any new relationship.

### 2. "Pesado" Prevention Strategy

Ivan's repeated apologies ("sorry if I'm too random") show active prevention of being seen as burden. He apologizes for:
- Texting
- Showing up
- Being random
- Being present

This is the "pesado" wound manifesting as preemptive damage control.

### 3. Group Context vs Individual

The exchange setting provided:
- Built-in group activities (less pressure on individual connections)
- Shared experiences (basketball, trips, parties)
- Natural context for service (cooking for the group)

Ivan may function better in group contexts where service is distributed.

### 4. Care During Crisis (Hospital Trip)

When Ideline needed hospital during SF trip, Ivan was present and supportive. This shows Ivan CAN show up for others' vulnerable moments. The issue isn't inability to care - it's inability to receive care.

### 5. Time-Limited Intensity

The exchange created an artificially intense 3-month friendship that then naturally ended. This structure might suit Ivan:
- Clear endpoint (less abandonment anxiety)
- Intense service period (The Fixer satisfied)
- Natural fade-out (no painful ending needed)

## Treatment Implications

### For Therapist:

1. **The Fixer as default:** Emerges within weeks in new relationships
2. **"Pesado" prevention:** Constant apologizing for presence
3. **Group contexts:** May be easier for Ivan than 1:1 intensity
4. **Can show up for others:** Hospital trip shows capacity for care-giving
5. **Time-limited relationships:** May feel safer (clear endpoint)

### Questions to Explore:

- Why does Ivan apologize for existing?
- Can Ivan sustain friendships without becoming "the helpful one"?
- What would a friendship look like where Ivan is NOT the primary service-provider?
- How did the exchange context (finite, group-based) serve Ivan's patterns?
