# Attachment Patterns: Overview

> **Assessment:** Complex, Context-Dependent  
> **Primary Pattern:** Varies by relational context  
> **Key Feature:** Different attachment styles emerge with different people

---

## Context-Dependent Attachment

Ivan's attachment style is not monolithic. It shifts based on context:

| Context | Attachment Style | Key Features |
|---------|------------------|--------------|
| **Family** | Dismissive-Avoidant | Information Firewall, emotional distance, surface-level interactions |
| **Intimate Partners** | Secure-leaning-Anxious | Desires closeness, but "pesado" wound creates anxiety about being "too much" |
| **Kink Contexts** | Most Secure | Permission structure allows authentic attachment needs to emerge without shame |

---

## With Family: Dismissive-Avoidant

### How It Looks

- The Information Firewall = systematic avoidance of emotional vulnerability
- Family knows logistics, not emotional life
- Keeps things light and happy
- Scans mood before speaking
- Strategic information management

### Why It Developed

- Learned early that sharing vulnerability leads to drama/shame (Lipstick Incident)
- Mother's unpredictability made openness unsafe
- Father's unavailability made emotional connection seem impossible
- Universal "pesado" labeling made needs feel pathological

### The Paradox

Ivan would call his mother in a medical emergency (trusts her competence) but wouldn't share the emotional experience (doesn't trust her reception).

---

## With Intimate Partners: Secure-Leaning-Anxious

### How It Looks

- Comfortable with intimacy
- Desires closeness and connection
- BUT: "pesado" wound creates anxiety about being "too much"
- May not express needs for fear of burdening
- Gives more than asks for in relationships

### The Anxious Edge

> *"Maybe there's doubt about whether those things are enough to make someone want to stay and give me the warmth I need."*

- Uncertainty about whether he deserves care
- Monitoring for signs he's being "too intense"
- May suppress needs preemptively

### What He Wants

> *"Someone who gives warmth, cuddles, sees me, doesn't treat my needs as annoying."*

---

## In Kink Contexts: Most Secure

### How It Looks

- Authentic attachment needs emerge freely
- Can ask for what he wants (as Brahm)
- Vulnerability feels safe
- Receives without needing to earn it

### Why It Works

The permission structure provides:

1. **Explicit safety** - Rules and roles are clear
2. **Named vulnerability** - "Being needy" is allowed, even desired
3. **Physical containment** - Regulated nervous system
4. **Consistent responses** - Predictable dynamics

> *"Brahm isn't allowed to be annoying in daily life. But in kink spaces, Brahm IS allowed to be inchabola and ask for head pats."*
